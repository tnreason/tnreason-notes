\section{Languages}

Languages over finite alphabets $\Sigma$ are collection of strings in $\Sigma^{*}$.
The symbols in $\Sigma$ are enumerated by an index interpretation map $\indexinterpretation$.

Collections $\arbset$ of strings of length $\indorder$ are represented by a boolean tensor
\begin{align*}
    \hypercoreofat{\arbset,\indorder}{\indvariableof{[\indorder]}}
    = \sum_{\imelement\in\arbset} \bigotimes_{\indenumeratorin}
    \onehotmapofat{\indexinterpretationat{\restrictionofto{\imelement}{\indenumerator}}}{\indvariableof{\indenumerator}} \, .
\end{align*}

A language $L$ is encoded by the infinite collection of tensors
\begin{align*}
    \{\hypercoreofat{L,\indorder}{\indvariableof{[\indorder]}} \wcols \indorder\in\nn \} \, ,
\end{align*}
each representing the set of strings of length $\indorder$.

We want to study how to decompose such tensors and relate tensor network schemes to standard models of computation.
