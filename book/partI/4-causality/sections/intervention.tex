\section{Intervention}

So far, there are several hypergraphs to model a probability distributions by a Bayesian Network.
We now want to understand the directed hyperedges as causal relationships between the variables, beyond encoding of conditional independencies (see \exaref{exa:patientTreatmentOutcome}).
To this end, we model the effects of interventions, which will distinguish between multiple Bayesian Networks modeling the same joint distribution.

\input{examples/patient_treatment_outcome}

Interventions on Bayesian Networks are modeled by the do-calculus \cite{pearl_causality_2009}, which we capture here based on do-variables.

\subsection{Do calculus}

We introduce the do-variables $\dovariableof{\catenumerator}$ taking values in $[\catdimof{\catenumerator}+1]$ for each variable $\catvariableof{\catenumerator}$ of dimension $\catdimof{\catenumerator}$, which model the effects of interventions.
The states are interpreted as
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\doindexof{\catenumerator}\in[\catdimof{\catenumerator}]$: An intervention setting $\catvariableof{\catenumerator}$ to the value $\doindexof{\catenumerator}$
    \item $\doindexof{\catenumerator}=\catdimof{\catenumerator}$: No intervention on $\catvariableof{\catenumerator}$
\end{itemize}

\begin{definition}
    The causally augmented conditional probability core is defined as
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\catvariableof{\atomenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}
        =& \condprobof{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}} \otimes \onehotmapofat{\catdimof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator}} \\
        &+ \sum_{\doindexof{\catenumerator}\in[\catdimof{\catenumerator}]} \onehotmapofat{\doindexof{\catenumerator}}{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}} \otimes \onehotmapofat{\doindexof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator}} \otimes \onesat{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}\, .
    \end{align*}
    The causally augmented Bayesian Network is the tensor network
    \begin{align*}
        \contractionof{
            \{\condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}} \wcols \catenumeratorin\}
        }{\shortcatvariables,\dovariableof{[\catorder]}} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{definition}

The Bayesian Networks investigated so far are retrieved by contractions with $\onehotmapofat{\catdimof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator}}$.
This corresponds with the situation, where no interventions are performed on any variable.

\begin{lemma}
    For any Bayesian Network $\probwith$ on a directed acyclic hypergraph $\graph=([\catorder],\edges)$ we have
    \begin{align*}
        \probwith =
        \contractionof{
            \{\condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}} \wcols \catenumeratorin\}
            \cup \{\onehotmapofat{\catdimof{\catenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator}} \wcols \catenumeratorin \}
        }{\shortcatvariables} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    By construction we have
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\catvariableof{\atomenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator}=\catdimof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}
        =& \condprobof{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}  \, .
    \end{align*}
    Performing this on each causally augmented conditional distribution retrieves thus the original Bayesian Network.
\end{proof}


Adding the do-variables comes with a moderate increase of the bas+ rank.
We provide the following bound.

\begin{lemma}
    For any conditional probability distribution we have
    \begin{align*}
        \bascprankof{\condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\catvariableof{\atomenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}}
        \leq \condprobat{\catvariableof{\atomenumerator}}{\dovariableof{\catenumerator},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}}
    \end{align*}
\end{lemma}

\subsection{Intervention Queries}

In a Bayesian Network, when intervening on a variable $\catvariableof{\node}$, the corresponding conditional probability gets trivialized.
The probability tensor is then captured by
\begin{align*}
    \condprobat{\catvariableof{\secnodes}}{\doof{\indexedcatvariableof{\thirdnodes}}}
    %  \condprobat{\catvariableof{\secnodes}}{\doof{X}}
    = \contractionof{
        \{\condprobof{\catvariableof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}} \,:\, \node\notin\thirdnodes \}
        \cup \{\frac{1}{\catdimof{\node}} \onesat{\catvariableof{\node},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}} \,:\, \node\in\thirdnodes\}
        \cup \{\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\node}} \,:\, \node\in\thirdnodes\}
    }{\catvariableof{\secnodes}} \, .
\end{align*}

Since $\frac{1}{\catdimof{\node}} \onesat{\catvariableof{\node},\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}}$ is directed with $\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}$ incoming and $\catvariableof{\node}$ outgoing, the partition function of the network stays $1$ and the normalization is captured by the contraction.

\subsection{Simplifications}

In general, when all variables are observable, intervention queries can be expressed by a collection of conditional queries (since any cpd of a Bayesian network is a conditional query, and we contract all except those with interventions on the head variables).
In realistic scenarios, however, not all variables are observable (for example, the smoking gene has been only assumed, but no observations have been made).
We are interested in situations, where intervention queries can be answered by conditional queries of observable variables.
We can proof them in the tensor network formalism based on network separations, which contribution to contractions are scalar multiplications, which therefore are dropped in normalizations.

An example for a simplification is given by the randomized controlled trial.

\subsubsection{Backdoor Criterion}

More general than in the randomized controlled trial we have to deal with confounding variables.
The Backdoor Criterion and the Frontdoor Criterion exploit mediator variables $\mediatorvar$, which are also observable and block certain causal paths to unobserved confounding variables $\confoundervar$.

\begin{definition}\label{def:backdoorCriterion}
    Let there be a directed acyclic hypergraph, such that $\intervenedvar$ is topologically ordered before $\observedvar$.
    A backdoor path is a path through the hypergraph, such that the first and the second node in the path are in the outgoing and the incoming nodes of a hyperedge.
    %If a variable from a set of variables $\mediatorvar$ is contained in
    If every backdoor path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$ is blocked by a variable in $\mediatorvar$, and $\mediatorvar$ does not contain a descendant of $\intervenedvar$, then we say $\mediatorvar$ satisfies the backdoor criterion with respect to $\intervenedvar$ and $\observedvar$.
\end{definition}

See \figref{fig:backdoorCriterion} for a typical hypergraph where $\mediatorvar$ satisfies the backdoor criterion.

\begin{lemma}\label{lem:bdCriterionMediatorCond}
    When $\mediatorvar$ satisfies the backdoor criterion for $\intervenedvar$ and $\observedvar$, and for some $\catindexofin{M},\catindexofin{\intervenedset}$ we have $\probat{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}\neq 0}$ then
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobat{\observedvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}}
        = \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    We denote $\arbset$ as the set of nodes appearing in backdoor trails from $\intervenedvar$ to $\mediatorvar$ and those variables having a variable in $\intervenedvar$ as a descendant.
    Since any backdoor path is blocked by $\mediatorvar$, we can isolate the contraction of the nodes in $\arbset$ by simplifying the contraction as
    \begin{align*}
        \probofat{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar,\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}}
        =
        &\contraction{
            \{\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\mediatorset}}{\mediatorvar}\} \cup
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}}
            \wcols \edge = (\{\node\},\incomingnodes) \ncond \node\in\arbset\ncond \node\notin \intervenedset \}
        } \\
        &\cdot \contractionof{
            \{\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\intervenedset}}{\intervenedvar},\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\mediatorset}}{\mediatorvar}\} \cup
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}}
            \wcols \edge = (\{\node\},\incomingnodes) \ncond \node\notin\arbset\ncond \node\notin \intervenedset \}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    We compare this contraction with the analogous decomposition
    \begin{align*}
        \probofat{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar,\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}}
        =
        &\contraction{
            \{\condprobat{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\intervenedset}}},\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\mediatorset}}{\mediatorvar}\} \cup
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}}
            \wcols \edge = (\{\node\},\incomingnodes) \ncond \node\in\arbset\ncond \node\notin \intervenedset \}
        } \\
        &\cdot \contractionof{
            \{\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\intervenedset}}{\intervenedvar},\onehotmapofat{\catindexof{\mediatorset}}{\mediatorvar}\} \cup
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\node}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\node}}}
            \wcols \edge = (\{\node\},\incomingnodes) \ncond \node\notin\arbset\ncond \node\notin \intervenedset \}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    and notice that both differ only in a factor.
    Whenever $\probat{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}}\neq 0$ this factor does not vanish.
    After normalization with $\intervenedvar$ and $\mediatorvar$ incoming, the tensors are therefore equivalent.
\end{proof}


\begin{lemma}\label{lem:bdCriterionDoInfluence}
    When $\mediatorvar$ does not contain a descendant of $\intervenedvar$ then
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\mediatorvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}}
        = \probat{\mediatorvar} \otimes \onesat{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    We causally augment the Bayesian network by the do variable $\dovariableof{\intervenedset}$, and notice that also with respect to the causally augmented hypergraph, $\mediatorvar$ does not contain a descendant of $\intervenedvar$ nor $\dovariableof{\intervenedset}$. %by adding $\dovariableof{\intervenedset}$ to the incoming nodes at the hyperedge with $\intervenedvar$ outgoing, and a decorated
    We then investigate for arbitrary $\doindexof{\intervenedset}\in[\catdimof{\intervenedset}+1]$ the contraction
    \begin{align*}
        \contractionof{
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}} \wcols \catenumeratorin\ncond\catenumerator\neq 0\}
            \cup \{\condprobat{\intervenedvar}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\intervenedset}}},\onehotmapofat{\doindexof{\intervenedset}}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}}\}
        }{\mediatorvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    We enumerate the nodes by a topological order, such that any node having $\mediatorvar$ as a descendant is ordered before those nodes ot having  $\mediatorvar$ as a descendant.
    For some $\catenumeratorin$ we then have that $\catvariableof{[\catenumerator]}$ are the variables having $\mediatorvar$ as a descendant and $\mediatorvar$ itself.
    \lemref{lem:bnSubContractionAsCondProb} implies for $\seccatenumerator=0$ and $\arbset=\varnothing$ that
    \begin{align*}
        \contractionof{
            \{\condprobat{\catvariableof{\catenumerator}}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\catenumerator}}} \wcols \catenumeratorin\ncond\catenumerator\neq 0\}
            \cup \{\condprobat{\intervenedvar}{\catvariableof{\parentsof{\intervenedset}}},\onehotmapofat{\doindexof{\intervenedset}}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}}\}
        }{\mediatorvar}
        = \contractionof{\condprobat{\catvariableof{[\catenumerator]}}{\varnothing}}{\mediatorvar}
        = \probat{\mediatorvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{proof}


\begin{theorem}[Backdoor Criterion]\label{the:backdoorCriterion}
    Let $\mediatorvar$ satisfy the backdoor criterion with respect to $\intervenedvar$ and $\observedvar$, no node in $\mediatorvar$ be a descendant of $\intervenedvar$, and let $\catindexofin{\intervenedset}$ be such that for any $\catindexofin{M}$ we have $\probat{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset}}\neq0$.
    Then we have
    %blocks any backdoor path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$ and no node in $\mediatorvar$ is a descendant of $\intervenedvar$, then
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobof{\observedvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\mediatorvar},
            \probat{\mediatorvar}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
    We have by the Bayes rule
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar,\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}},
            \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\mediatorvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    We apply \lemref{lem:bdCriterionMediatorCond} for arbitrary $\catindexofin{M}$ and get
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset},\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \condprobat{\observedvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\mediatorset},\indexeddovariableof{\intervenedset}} \, .
    \end{align*}
    By assumption of the backdoor criterion, $\mediatorvar$ is not a descendant of $\intervenedvar$.
    \lemref{lem:bdCriterionDoInfluence} thus implies
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\mediatorvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \probat{\mediatorvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    We conclude that
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobof{\observedvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset},\mediatorvar},
            \probat{\mediatorvar}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
\end{proof}

\subsubsection{Frontdoor Criterion}

We now state the frontdoor criterion, see \cite{pearl_causal_2016}.

\begin{definition}
    A frontdoor path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$ is any directed path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$.
    A set $\mediatorvar$ of variables satisfies the frontdoor criterion with respect to $\intervenedvar$ and $\observedvar$, if the following hold:
    \begin{itemize}
        \item Any frontdoor path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$ contains a node in $\mediatorvar$.
        \item There is no backdoor path from $\intervenedvar$ to $\mediatorvar$.
        \item All backdoor paths from $\mediatorvar$ to $\observedvar$ are blocked by $\intervenedvar$.
    \end{itemize}
\end{definition}


\begin{lemma}[Frontdoor Criterion]
    When $\mediatorvar$ satisfies the frontdoor criterion with respect to $\intervenedvar$ and $\observedvar$, %and there is no confounder between $\intervenedvar$ and $\mediatorvar$, as well as between $\mediatorvar$ and $\observedvar$, -> Definition
    then
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobof{\mediatorvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset}},
            \contractionof{\condprobof{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar,\intervenedvar},\probat{\intervenedvar}}{\observedvar,\mediatorvar}
%            \condprobof{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar,\tildecatvariableof{\intervenedset}},
%            \probat{\tildecatvariableof{\intervenedset}}
        }{\observedvar} \, .
    \end{align*}
    Note that the inner contraction has $\intervenedvar$ as a closed variable.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
    We expand
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\mediatorvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}},
            \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar,\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        }{\observedvar}
    \end{align*}
    Since there are no backdoor trails from $\intervenedvar$ to $\mediatorvar$, we apply the backdoor simplification by \theref{the:backdoorCriterion} to get
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\mediatorvar}{\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \condprobat{\mediatorvar}{\indexedcatvariableof{\intervenedset}} \, .
    \end{align*}
    Since $\mediatorvar$ blocks all directed paths from $\intervenedvar$ to $\observedvar$ we have for any $\catindexofin{\mediatorset}$
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar=\catindexof{\mediatorset},\dovariableof{\intervenedset}=\catindexof{\intervenedset}}
        = \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\mediatorset}=\catindexof{\mediatorset}} \, .
    \end{align*}
    By assumption, $\intervenedvar$ further satisfies the backdoor criterion with respect to $\mediatorvar$ and $\observedvar$.
    We can therefore apply the backdoor simplification by \theref{the:backdoorCriterion} again and get
    \begin{align*}
        \condprobwrtof{\causalsymbol}{\observedvar}{\dovariableof{\mediatorset}=\catindexof{\mediatorset}}
        = \contractionof{
            \condprobat{\observedvar}{\mediatorvar,\intervenedvar},\probat{\intervenedvar}
        }{\observedvar}\, .
    \end{align*}
    Combining these contraction identities, we arrive at the claim.

\end{proof}
